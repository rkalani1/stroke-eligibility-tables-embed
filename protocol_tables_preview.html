Ischemic Stroke - Acute (Onset ≤ 24 Hours)

Study Hypothesis / Summary Eligibility Key Exclusions
SISTER ↗
Enrolling 		A Phase-2, prospective, randomized, placebo-controlled, blinded, dose finding trial determining the safety and preliminary efficacy of TS23 (monoclonal antibody against alpha-2 antiplasmin) in acute ischemic stroke.
  • Anterior circulation acute ischemic stroke
  • Within 4.5 - 24 hours of onset
  • NIHSS ≥ 4
  • ASPECTS ≥ 6 on CT
  • Received thrombolysis or EVT with clot engagement
  • Known stroke in past 90 days
  • Pre-stroke mRS > 2
STEP ↗
Enrolling 		Randomized, Multifactorial, Adaptive, Platform trial optimizing care for AIS due to large or medium vessel occlusions (LVOs and MVOs).
Eligible Populations:
  • LVO Population: Complete occlusion of intracranial ICA or M1 MCA, with mild deficits (NIHSS 0 - 5)
  • MVO Population: Non-dominant or Co-dominant M2 and M3 MCA occlusions, with NIHSS ≥ 8
  • CT ASPECTS < 6
  • Acute occlusions in multiple vascular territories or tandem occlusions
TESTED ↗
Enrolling 		A prospective, observational study for persons with a pre-stroke modified Rankin Scale (mRS) 3-4 experiencing an LVO-AIS, comparing the effectiveness of EVT to medical management.
  • AIS presenting within 24 hours of onset
  • Occlusion of ICA, M1, or dominant M2
  • Pre-stroke mRS 3 - 4
  • NIHSS ≥ 6
  • Terminal illness at time of stroke
  • Stroke onset during acute inpatient facility stay

Ischemic Stroke - Inpatient (Admission to Day 30)

Study Hypothesis / Summary Eligibility Key Exclusions
VERIFY ↗
Enrolling 		Validating CMS structure/function measures (TMS motor evoked potentials and MRI lesion load) to establish early prognostic data for upper extremity recovery outcomes.
  • Unilaterally symptomatic acute ischemic stroke with motor deficits
  • Consented within 24 - 96 hours of LNW
  • Pre-stroke upper extremity condition limiting use
  • Dense sensory loss (NIHSS score = 2) or seizure since onset
SCOUTS-3 ↗
Enrolling 		Evaluating an intensive CPAP support intervention to improve adherence and recovery in stroke survivors with Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA) undergoing inpatient rehab.
  • Acute ischemic stroke within past 30 days
  • Admitted or moving to designated inpatient rehabilitation
  • Pregnancy, incarceration, tracheostomy, or CPAP use within 14 days pre-stroke
CLARITY ↗
Soon 		Phase 3 trial evaluating cilostazol addition to standard single antiplatelet therapy for secondary prevention in non-cardioembolic stroke/TIA.
  • Non-cardioembolic ischemic stroke (no ABCD2 check) or high-risk TIA (requires ABCD2 ≥ 6) within 180 days
  • Age ≥ 40 years
  • Cardioembolic stroke source
  • Contraindication to cilostazol (Congestive Heart Failure)
ESUS MRI
Enrolling 		Evaluating cerebellar and cardiac vessel wall MRI to reclassify ESUS patients into cardioembolic or large artery atherosclerosis etiologies.
  • Acute ischemic stroke (ESUS, cardioembolic, or large artery)
  • Scan possible within 6 months of stroke onset
  • Age < 35 years or eGFR < 35
  • Contraindication to gadolinium-contrast MRI
MOCHA
Enrolling 		Evaluating intracranial vessel wall MRI combined with clinical workups to identify stroke etiology in ESUS patients.
  • Acute ischemic stroke of ESUS etiology
  • Scan possible within 4 months of stroke onset
  • Contraindication to MRI or history of bilateral carotid revascularization
INTERCEPT ↗
Soon 		Safety and efficacy evaluation of bilateral carotid filters as an adjunct to Oral Anticoagulation in atrial fibrillation patients.
  • Ischemic stroke occurred within the past year
  • History of clinical Atrial Fibrillation on OAC
  • History of spontaneous ICH
  • ≥ 50% stenosis of carotid, subclavian, vertebral or intracranial arteries

Intracranial Hemorrhage (ICH) - Acute (Onset ≤ 24 Hours)

Study Hypothesis / Summary Eligibility Key Exclusions
MINUTE ↗
Soon 		Prospective, randomized trial evaluating the clinical utility of ultra-early SCUBA neuroendoscopic evacuation of Basal Ganglia Hemorrhages.
  • Basal Ganglia hemorrhage ≥ 20mL
  • Within 16 hours of last known well
  • NIHSS ≥ 6
  • Suspected secondary cause for ICH
  • Infratentorial or thalamic hemorrhage
  • GCS score < 7 at presentation
SATURN ↗
Enrolling 		Evaluating continuation vs discontinuation of statins on lobar ICH recurrence.
  • Spontaneous lobar ICH
  • Can be randomized within 7 days of onset
  • Age ≥ 50 years, taking statin at onset
  • ICH score > 3 or pre-stroke mRS > 3
  • Suspected secondary cause for ICH

Enrolling 		Evaluating if apixaban is superior to aspirin for stroke prevention in patients with recent ICH and non-valvular atrial fibrillation.

  • Suspected secondary cause for ICH
  • Clear ongoing therapeutic indication for anticoagulation/antiplatelet
